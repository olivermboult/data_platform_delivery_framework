# Project Finance

## Capture Budget and Financial Authority

**Activities**
- Capture the total budget available for the project and any constraints on how it can be used.
- Identify what costs the budget is expected to cover, including internal resource time, licensing, and infrastructure, and document any areas where coverage is unclear.
- Capture whether the budget has been formally approved and document whether any portion has been set aside for contingency.
- Capture whether further phases are anticipated beyond the initial delivery and whether budget has been set aside for these.
- Identify who holds budget authority for the project and document the approvals process for committing spend.
- Capture the customer's process for managing financial changes or variations during the project, including who has authority to approve budget adjustments.

**Outcomes**
- Documented total project budget and any constraints on how it can be used.
- Documented scope of budget coverage, including any areas of uncertainty.
- Documented approval status of the budget and any contingency set aside.
- Documented whether further phases are anticipated and whether budget has been allocated for these.
- Identified budget authority holder and documented approvals process for committing spend.
- Documented process for managing financial changes or variations, including sign-off authority.

**Pre-requisites**
- A stakeholder with budget authority or financial responsibility for the project is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of stakeholders involved in budget authority and approvals
- Complexity of the approvals process for committing spend
- Extent of budget ambiguity or unclear coverage

## Identify Financial Constraints and Timing

**Activities**
- Identify any financial year-end dates or budget cycle constraints that could affect when spend can be committed.
- Identify any budget constraints that could affect the scope, phasing, or timeline of delivery.
- Capture the customer's payment terms and invoicing requirements, and identify any approval or processing cycles that should be factored into project scheduling.

**Outcomes**
- Documented financial year-end dates and budget cycle constraints affecting spend timing.
- Documented budget constraints that could affect scope, phasing, or timeline.
- Documented payment terms, invoicing requirements, and any approval or processing cycles affecting project scheduling.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Capture Budget and Financial Authority task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of budget cycle or financial year-end constraints
- Number of invoicing or payment processing cycles to consider

---

## Identify Commercial Context and Procurement Requirements

**Activities**
- Identify any existing contracts or licensing agreements that could affect costs or constrain provider options.
- Identify any procurement or legal processes that must be followed before committing spend.
- Capture whether the customer is required to use an approved supplier list or procurement framework, and document the relevant requirements if so.

**Outcomes**
- Documented existing contracts and licensing agreements that could affect costs or constrain provider options.
- Documented procurement and legal processes that must be followed before committing spend.
- Documented approved supplier list or procurement framework requirements where applicable.

**Pre-requisites**
- A contact with knowledge of the customer's procurement and commercial arrangements is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of existing contracts or licensing arrangements
- Number of procurement processes to be followed
- Whether an approved supplier list or framework applies

---

## Identify Financial Risks and Assumptions

**Activities**
- Identify any financial risks or dependencies, such as licensing costs that may vary by provider or costs contingent on third-party access.
- Identify and document any cost assumptions that need to be validated before progressing to design.

**Outcomes**
- Documented financial risks and dependencies, including any costs that are variable or contingent.
- Documented cost assumptions requiring validation before progressing to design.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number and complexity of financial risks or dependencies identified
- Number of cost assumptions requiring validation
