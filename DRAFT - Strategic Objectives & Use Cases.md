# Design - Use Cases & Strategic Roadmap

## Strategic Objectives

- Confirm that the strategic objectives have not changed since Discovery, and document any updates where the position has shifted.

## Use Case Register

- Compile all confirmed use cases from Discovery into a single register, with each entry documenting the pain points addressed, expected business benefits, agreed success criteria and KPIs, the defined scope boundary, and any assumptions or dependencies recorded during Discovery.
- Confirm with the relevant stakeholders that each use case definition, scope boundary, and success criteria remain accurate since Discovery, and update any entries where the position has changed.
- Confirm the prioritised order of use cases still reflects current business priorities, regulatory or contractual drivers, and any inter-use-case dependencies.
- Confirm that each use case remains within the budget and timeline boundaries agreed during Discovery, and flag any that have grown in scope or complexity.
- Document any use cases that were considered but descoped during or since Discovery, along with the reason, to provide a clear record for future phases.

## Delivery Phases

- Group confirmed use cases into delivery phases, reflecting their priority order, inter-use-case dependencies, and the capacity available across each phase.
- Assign an expected delivery period to each phase, expressed as a date range or quarter.
- Confirm any dependencies between phases that would affect the sequencing or start date of later phases.

## Value Realisation

- Document the expected business benefits to be realised at the completion of each delivery phase, linking each benefit to the relevant use cases.
- Confirm the individual or team responsible for tracking and reporting on value realisation following each phase.

## Programme Risks

- Document any risks that span multiple use cases or affect the programme as a whole, including risks related to data availability, organisational readiness, third-party dependencies, or budget.
- Confirm the approach for reviewing and escalating programme-level risks throughout delivery.

## Programme Milestones

- Define the key programme-level milestones, tied to the delivery of business value rather than technical completion events.
- Confirm the sign-off or evidence required to mark each milestone as complete.

## Sign-off

- Obtain formal sign-off on the completed Strategic Roadmap from the Project Sponsor before any other Design activity produces substantive output.
