# Platform Organisation & Capacity

## Review Existing Capacity and Licensing

**Activities**
- Identify any existing capacity reservations or commitments, and document any constraints these impose on the project.
- Document the current licensing model and subscription tier in use, and identify whether either is expected to change as part of the project.
- Document the number and size of existing capacities, including how they are structured and when they are typically scaled, paused, or reserved.
- Identify whether capacities are currently shared across environments, and document any preferences or constraints the customer has regarding capacity sharing.
- Identify any performance issues or capacity limitations the customer has experienced with the current setup.
- Identify any cost or budget constraints related to capacity provisioning.

**Outcomes**
- Documented existing capacity reservations or commitments and any constraints they impose on the project.
- Documented current licensing model and subscription tier, and whether changes are expected.
- Documented number and size of existing capacities, including structure and scaling behaviour.
- Documented whether capacities are shared across environments, and customer preferences or constraints regarding capacity sharing.
- Documented performance issues or capacity limitations experienced with the current setup.
- Documented cost or budget constraints related to capacity provisioning.

**Pre-requisites**
- A technical contact with knowledge of existing capacity and licensing arrangements is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of existing capacities in use
- Complexity of scaling and sharing arrangements
- Extent of known performance or capacity limitations

## Review Workspace and Domain Structure

**Activities**
- Document how existing workspaces are structured and managed, including any known strengths and pain points of the current approach.
- Document how existing domains are structured and managed, including any known strengths and pain points of the current approach.
- Identify the number of workspaces currently in use and their general purpose or ownership.
- Identify any existing naming conventions for capacities, workspaces, and domains, and capture whether the customer has any requirements or preferences regarding their continuation.
- Identify whether there are any tenant-level settings or policies that may restrict how workspaces or domains can be configured.
- Capture any requirements or preferences the customer has regarding how workspaces and domains should be structured for the project.

**Outcomes**
- Documented existing workspace structure and management approach, including known strengths and pain points.
- Documented existing domain structure and management approach, including known strengths and pain points.
- Documented number of workspaces currently in use and their general purpose or ownership.
- Documented existing naming conventions for capacities, workspaces, and domains, and customer preferences regarding continuation.
- Documented tenant-level settings or policies that may restrict workspace or domain configuration.
- Documented customer requirements and preferences for workspace and domain structure for the project.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Review Existing Capacity and Licensing task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of workspaces and domains currently in use
- Complexity of tenant-level settings or policies
- Extent of existing documentation available

## Identify Platform Administration and Access Responsibilities

**Activities**
- Identify who holds administrative access to capacities, workspaces, and domains, and document whether this is expected to change as part of the project.
- Identify who currently manages capacity and licensing, and document whether they will be available to support the project.
- Capture the process for provisioning or modifying access to workspaces and capacities, and identify who has the authority to approve changes.

**Outcomes**
- Identified administrators of capacities, workspaces, and domains, and documented whether administrative access is expected to change.
- Identified individuals responsible for capacity and licensing management, and documented their availability to support the project.
- Documented process for provisioning or modifying access to workspaces and capacities, including approval authority.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Review Workspace and Domain Structure task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of administrators across capacities, workspaces, and domains
- Complexity of access provisioning processes
