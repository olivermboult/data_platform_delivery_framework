# Subject Matter Expert

The Subject Matter Expert is a senior operational representative within a specific business area, typically a department manager or senior analyst who understands in detail how their area of the business works. They are the primary source of business process and use case context throughout Discovery, providing the knowledge of day to day operations, workflows, and business logic that underpins design decisions. Where a Data Steward provides detailed knowledge of data structure and quality, and a Business User provides frontline experience of reporting consumption, the Subject Matter Expert bridges the two connecting business processes to the data and outputs the solution needs to support. There will typically be multiple Subject Matter Experts on a project, each engaged independently for their specific business area.

**Framework Involvement**

Pre-Discovery
- Provides business context on potential use cases within their area, including the problems they address and the expected outcomes.
- Confirms the relevance and feasibility of identified use cases against day to day operational reality within their area. The Customer Data Engineer or Technical Stakeholder is the named contact responsible for providing source system detail during Platform, Data and AI Initiation.

Discovery
- Acts as the primary source of detail on individual use cases and business processes during Use Case Discovery, providing the operational knowledge needed to define pain points, success criteria, and business logic accurately.
- Validates that use case definitions accurately reflect how their area of the business works before outputs are finalised.
- Contributes business process context to Business-Data Alignment sessions, helping confirm that the data identified for each use case is appropriate and that transformation assumptions are grounded in operational reality.
- Confirms the meaning and expected behaviour of data within source systems during Source System Discovery, providing context that supports accurate data profiling and quality assessment.
- Contributes to the definition of reporting requirements within their area during Reporting Initiation and Business-Data Alignment, providing context on what questions reports must answer and what information is most valuable to their team.

Design
- Remains available to the delivery team to clarify business logic, validate transformation assumptions, and confirm that design decisions accurately reflect operational requirements within their area.
- Reviews reporting wireframes for their area and confirms that the information hierarchy, metrics, and narrative meet the needs of their team before build begins.

Build
- Remains available to the Data Engineer to clarify business rules and transformation logic within their area where queries arise during build.
- Validates transformation outputs against business expectations where queries arise that require operational context to resolve.

Quality Assurance
- Where assigned based on the complexity of the use cases, participates in UAT to validate that the solution accurately reflects the business processes and logic described during Discovery.
- Confirms that outputs within their area meet the success criteria agreed during Discovery.

Live
- Participates in the post project review, providing input on whether the solution accurately reflects the business processes and requirements described during Discovery.
