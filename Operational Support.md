# Operational Support

## Capture Support Arrangement Requirements

**Activities**
- Document the customer's expectations for how the solution will be supported after go-live, including whether this will be handled internally, externally, or through a combination of both.
- Capture whether a managed service or ongoing consultancy arrangement is expected, and document any preferences or constraints around how this would operate.
- Identify whether support is expected outside standard business hours and document any critical periods where elevated availability is required.
- Capture any existing support frameworks, tools, or processes the customer currently uses to manage operational issues.

**Outcomes**
- Documented customer expectations for post-go-live support, including whether support will be internal, external, or combined.
- Documented preferences and constraints around any managed service or consultancy arrangement.
- Documented out-of-hours support requirements and any critical periods requiring elevated availability.
- Documented existing support frameworks, tools, and processes currently in use.

**Pre-requisites**
- Named stakeholders with responsibility for operational decisions are available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of the expected support model
- Number of stakeholders required to define support arrangements
- Extent of existing support frameworks or tooling in place

## Identify Support Responsibilities and Contacts

**Activities**
- Identify the primary point of contact for support queries on the customer side, and document their role and availability.
- Map the escalation path for issues that cannot be resolved at first contact, and identify who is involved at each stage.
- Identify who will be responsible for ongoing maintenance, platform updates, and dependency changes after go-live.
- Identify any third-party platforms or services in scope that carry their own support arrangements, and capture the terms and limitations of those arrangements.
- Capture the customer's expectations around the handover of support responsibilities at go-live, including any requirements for how this should be documented or formally agreed.
- Identify where the boundary sits between the data team's support responsibilities and those of any wider IT or infrastructure function, and document any areas of ambiguity.

**Outcomes**
- Identified primary point of contact for support queries, including their role and availability.
- Documented escalation path for issues, including individuals involved at each stage.
- Identified individuals or teams responsible for ongoing maintenance, platform updates, and dependency changes after go-live.
- Documented third-party support arrangements for platforms and services in scope, including terms and limitations.
- Documented customer expectations for the handover of support responsibilities at go-live.
- Documented boundary between data team support responsibilities and wider IT or infrastructure functions, including any areas of ambiguity.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Capture Support Arrangement Requirements task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of third-party platforms or services with their own support arrangements
- Complexity of escalation paths and responsibility boundaries
- Number of stakeholders involved in support responsibilities

## Capture Service Level Requirements

**Activities**
- Capture the customer's expectations around response and resolution times for different categories of issue, including any existing service level benchmarks or precedents they apply.
- Capture how the customer currently categorises or prioritises operational issues, including any severity levels or priority tiers they apply.
- Capture whether proactive monitoring and alerting are expected as part of the support scope, and document which systems, data flows, or outcomes the customer expects to be covered.
- Identify any constraints in the customer's current resources, processes, or tooling that could limit their ability to operate the support model independently after go-live.
- Identify any downstream SLA commitments or reporting deadlines that the support model must accommodate.

**Outcomes**
- Documented customer expectations for response and resolution times across issue categories.
- Documented issue categorisation and priority tiers currently in use.
- Documented requirements for proactive monitoring and alerting within the support scope, including systems and outcomes to be covered.
- Documented constraints on the customer's ability to operate the support model independently after go-live.
- Documented downstream SLA commitments and reporting deadlines the support model must accommodate.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Capture Support Arrangement Requirements task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of service level requirements and issue categorisation
- Number of downstream SLA commitments or reporting deadlines
- Extent of known resource or tooling constraints
