# Design - Project Governance

## Governance and Decision-Making

- Confirm the project governance framework for the Delivery stage, including how progress will be tracked, how decisions will be made, and who holds authority for different categories of decision.
- Confirm the escalation path for decisions that cannot be resolved within the delivery team, and ensure this is understood by all relevant parties before build begins.
- Confirm any governance requirements specific to the customer's organisation, such as steering committee reviews or board-level reporting, and agree how these will be accommodated throughout delivery.
- Confirm the availability of key stakeholders throughout the Delivery stage, and identify any periods of unavailability that may affect decision-making or delivery progress.

## Communication and Reporting

- Confirm the meeting cadence for the Delivery stage, including the frequency and format of standups, progress reviews, and steering meetings.
- Confirm the schedule, format, and audience for project status reporting throughout delivery.
- Confirm the communication channels and the individuals who should be included in project communications throughout delivery.

## Delivery Sequence

- Define the phased delivery roadmap, sequencing the Platform, Build, and Reporting phases and confirming the expected start and end dates for each.
- Confirm the sequencing of use cases within the Build phase, reflecting the priorities confirmed in the Strategic Roadmap and any technical dependencies between workstreams.
- Identify any dependencies between delivery workstreams that affect sequencing, and confirm how these will be managed to avoid delays.
- Identify any external dependencies, such as third-party access, customer provisioning tasks, or regulatory approvals, that could affect the delivery timeline, and confirm the agreed approach for managing these.
- Confirm the timeline for Quality Assurance and Release following the completion of the Delivery stage, and ensure these are reflected in the overall project timeline.

## Delivery Milestones

- Define the key milestones throughout delivery at which progress will be formally assessed, confirming the deliverable or sign-off required at each.

## Risk and Change

- Confirm the process for identifying, logging, and managing risks and issues during delivery, and confirm who holds responsibility at each step.
- Confirm the process for assessing and approving changes to scope, timeline, or budget during delivery, including the authority levels required for different categories of change.
- Confirm the approach to change management throughout delivery, defining how changes to scope, timeline, or resource will be assessed, approved, and reflected in the plan.

## Resource Plan

- Confirm the full resource plan for the Delivery stage, detailing the roles required, the phases in which each role is active, and the estimated effort for each.
- Identify any specialist roles required for specific phases or workstreams, such as the AI/ML Engineer, I&S Engineer, or Test Engineer, and confirm their availability and the point at which they will be engaged.
- Confirm the approach to resourcing continuity throughout delivery, identifying any planned absences, capacity constraints, or handover requirements that could affect the delivery team's ability to maintain progress.
