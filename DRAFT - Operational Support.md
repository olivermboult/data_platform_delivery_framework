# Design - Support Model

## Support Arrangement

- Document the agreed support arrangement, confirming whether the solution will be supported internally, externally, or through a combination of both, and detailing the responsibilities on each side.
- Document the categories of issue the support model will cover, such as incident management, bug fixes, performance degradation, and planned maintenance.
- Confirm whether support is required outside standard business hours and document the agreed availability, including any critical periods where heightened availability is required.
- Confirm whether a managed service or ongoing consultancy arrangement is in place and ensure this is reflected in the project scope and commercial terms.

## Responsibilities and Escalation

- Confirm the primary point of contact for support queries on the customer side and ensure they are aware of this responsibility before go-live.
- Document the escalation path for issues that cannot be resolved at first contact, confirming who is involved at each stage.
- Confirm the stabilisation period arrangement immediately following go-live, defining the level of involvement the delivery team will maintain and the point at which responsibility transfers fully to the customer.
- Confirm who is responsible for ongoing maintenance, platform updates, and dependency changes after go-live.
- Identify any third-party platforms or services in scope that carry their own support arrangements and document how these interact with the overall support model.

## Service Levels

- Document the agreed response and resolution times for each category of issue as the governing targets for the support arrangement.
- Confirm the incident management process, detailing how issues will be raised, logged, triaged, and resolved, and confirm who holds responsibility at each step.
- Confirm whether proactive monitoring and alerting are included in the support scope and document what these cover.
- Identify any gaps in the customer's internal capability that would prevent them from operating the support model independently, and confirm how these will be addressed before go-live.
