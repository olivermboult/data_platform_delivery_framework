# Discovery

Discovery spans from project kick-off to a documented view of the customer's data landscape, business needs, and future priorities.

The phase establishes scope, confirms constraints and risks, and builds the business, technical, and organisational picture needed to design the solution. Use cases are defined and prioritised with stakeholders, the current environment and source systems are assessed in depth, and data and reporting design is based on available data before any technical work begins.

The phase closes with a discovery report, high-level architecture, and initial project plan. These form the foundation on which Design is built.

## Discovery Areas

### Business & Operations
Business & Operations establishes the strategic and organisational context of the project. Use cases are identified, refined, and prioritised with stakeholders. Governance structure, data ownership responsibilities, budget position, team capabilities, and post-go-live support requirements are also confirmed.

### Data & AI
Data & AI covers the existing data landscape and, where in scope, AI workloads. Source systems, data architecture, data quality, and data models are assessed. Where AI workloads are in scope, readiness, tooling, model development requirements, MLOps practices, and governance are also documented.

### Infrastructure & Security
Infrastructure & Security covers the technical environment in which the solution will operate. The existing platform, access controls, compliance obligations, data protection requirements, performance characteristics, disaster recovery posture, and deployment practices are assessed and documented.

### Reporting & Analysis
Reporting & Analysis covers the existing reporting landscape and the requirements for the new solution. Current tools, usage patterns, performance issues, and migration scope are assessed. Storyboards are produced to validate reporting requirements against agreed use cases before Design begins.

## Entry Requirements

- The Head of Consultancy or the Data Lead has reviewed the initial brief and confirmed it contains sufficient information to proceed with the phase
- The Project Sponsor has been confirmed as the named customer contact with authority to make decisions on scope, priorities, and budget
- The customer has confirmed availability for the required sessions and signed off the estimated time and cost of the phase
- Relevant Technical Stakeholders, Subject Matter Experts, Data Owners and Stewards, and Business Users have been confirmed on the customer side and are available to participate in discovery sessions
- A Data Lead and any other required roles have been allocated with the capacity to conduct the phase

## Exit Requirements

- Use cases have been defined in full, including problem statements, success measures, and KPIs, and the prioritised list has been formally signed off by the Project Sponsor
- Each confirmed use case has data mappings, high-level data models, and reporting storyboards that have been validated against agreed requirements
- The wider business and operations requirements have been documented
- The technical landscape in scope has been assessed and documented
- The data landscape in scope has been assessed and documented
- The reporting landscape in scope has been assessed and documented
- Where AI workloads are in scope, the AI readiness assessment, model specifications, tooling preferences, MLOps requirements, and AI governance position have been documented
- A discovery report, high-level architecture, initial project plan, and risk log with mitigation plans have been produced
- All Discovery outputs have been signed off by the Project Sponsor and key stakeholders, with internal sign-off obtained from the Head of Consultancy, before progressing to Design

## Roles

*Note - a person may have multiple roles within a project*

| Role | Side | Involvement |
|---|---|---|
| Data Lead | Consultancy | Accountable for the phase and all outputs; provides oversight across all activities, leads finance and governance conversations, and signs off all outputs internally before submission to the customer |
| Data Consultant | Consultancy | Leads all discovery activities and is responsible for facilitating sessions and producing discovery outputs |
| Data Engineer | Consultancy | Contributes technical input across Infrastructure & Security, Data & AI, and Reporting & Analysis where assigned|
| Reporting Developer | Consultancy | May lead or support Reporting & Analysis where assigned |
| Business Analyst | Consultancy | May lead or support Business & Operations sessions where assigned |
| I&S Engineer | Consultancy | Contributes specialist input across Infrastructure & Security where assigned |
| AI/ML Engineer | Consultancy | May lead or support Data & AI activities and contributes specialist input to AI model development and MLOps where AI workloads are in scope and the role is assigned |
| Project Sponsor | Customer | Primary source of information for Project Kick-Off, confirming strategic alignment and commercial context; approves use case priorities and scope throughout the phase and signs off the prioritised use case list and all Discovery outputs |
| Technical Stakeholder | Customer | Primary source of information across all infrastructure, security, and platform subjects throughout the phase |
| Customer Data Engineer | Customer | Provides technical detail on existing pipelines, platform configuration, source system access, and deployment practices |
| Data Owner | Customer | Primary source of information on data ownership, domain accountability, and data availability across relevant domains |
| Data Steward | Customer | Provides detailed knowledge of data structure, quality, and business rules within their domain |
| Subject Matter Expert | Customer | Consulted across activities for operational detail, use case context, and validation of business logic |
| Business User | Customer | Consulted on use case detail, reporting needs, and frontline operational context |
