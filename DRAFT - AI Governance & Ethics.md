# Design - AI Governance & Ethics

## Governance Framework

- Define the governance framework to be applied to all AI models in the solution, covering model documentation requirements, review and approval processes, and the controls in place to ensure outputs are transparent, fair, and explainable.
- Confirm the transparency, fairness, and explainability requirements identified during Discovery, and specify how these will be addressed in the design of each AI use case.
- Confirm the process for reviewing and approving AI model outputs before they reach production, and document who holds sign-off authority at each stage.
- Confirm the AI acceptable use policy is in place and that responsibility for maintaining it has been assigned, or document the steps required to establish one before build begins.

## Bias and Human Oversight

- Confirm the bias risks identified during Discovery for each use case, and specify the approach to identifying, measuring, and mitigating bias throughout model development and monitoring.
- Confirm which use cases require human oversight or intervention mechanisms, as identified during Discovery, and specify how these will be implemented within the solution.

## Regulatory Obligations

- Confirm the regulatory obligations identified during Discovery, and document how each is addressed within the governance framework.
- Confirm whether data protection obligations apply to the use of personal data in AI model training or inference, and document the steps taken to meet these obligations, including any involvement of the Data Protection Officer.
- Specify the model documentation and audit trail requirements confirmed during Discovery, including any requirements for model cards, performance logs, or records of AI-assisted decisions.

## Third-Party Tools and Stakeholder Sign-off

- Confirm the governance requirements that apply to any third-party AI tools or services identified during Discovery, and specify how these will be observed within the solution.
- Confirm that the AI governance framework has been reviewed and approved by the relevant stakeholders, including the customer's data protection officer or equivalent and any compliance or legal function, before build begins.
