# Design - AI Readiness

## Data Readiness

- Confirm that the data available for each AI use case meets the requirements defined during Discovery, covering volume, completeness, and consistency, and document any outstanding gaps and the agreed approach for addressing them before model development begins.
- Confirm how any data sensitivity or regulatory restrictions identified during Discovery are reflected in the AI design, including any requirements for data anonymisation, access controls, or usage boundaries.
- Where supervised learning use cases are in scope, confirm the data labelling approach, including how labelling will be resourced and quality-controlled.

## Platform and Capability Readiness

- Confirm that the chosen platform and infrastructure are capable of supporting the intended AI workloads, and flag any constraints that must be accounted for in the design.
- Confirm that the process changes identified during Discovery as necessary to accompany the technical delivery are documented and a communication plan is in place for the relevant stakeholders before build begins.
- Confirm that the customer has the internal capability to support and maintain AI workloads after delivery, and ensure any skills gaps identified during Discovery are reflected in the Skills & Capabilities design.
