# Data Ownership

## Identify Data Domains and Ownership Responsibilities

**Activities**
- Identify all data domains relevant to the project scope.
- Identify the named Data Owner and Data Steward for each domain.
- Identify any domains where ownership is unclear, contested, or has not yet been assigned, and document the risk this presents to delivery.
- Document the responsibilities of each Data Owner and Data Steward, and identify any areas where roles are unclear or not yet fully defined.
- Identify whether data ownership responsibilities will change as a result of the project, for example where new data domains are introduced or existing ones are restructured.
- Identify the escalation path for ownership disputes or decisions that require cross-domain agreement.

**Outcomes**
- Documented list of data domains relevant to the project scope.
- Identified named Data Owner and Data Steward for each domain.
- Documented domains where ownership is unclear, contested, or unassigned, including associated delivery risks.
- Documented responsibilities of each Data Owner and Data Steward, including any gaps or areas of ambiguity.
- Identified changes to data ownership responsibilities expected as a result of the project.
- Documented escalation path for ownership disputes or cross-domain decisions.

**Pre-requisites**
- A stakeholder with knowledge of the organisation's data governance structure is available for a workshop.
- Named Data Owners and Data Stewards are available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of data domains in scope
- Maturity of the customer's data governance structure
- Clarity of existing role definitions
- Complexity of cross-domain ownership arrangements
- Number of stakeholders required for workshop

## Establish the Data Governance Context

**Activities**
- Identify whether a data governance framework or policy is in place, and document any gaps relevant to the project.
- Identify whether a data catalogue or business glossary exists, and document its scope and currency where one is in place.
- Identify whether a data governance committee or forum exists, and document its remit and membership.
- Document the process for raising and resolving data quality or governance issues, and identify who holds accountability at each step.
- Identify which data domains are subject to regulatory or compliance requirements, and document the individuals accountable for meeting those obligations.

**Outcomes**
- Documented data governance framework or policy in place, including any gaps relevant to the project.
- Documented data catalogue or business glossary in place, including scope and currency where one exists.
- Documented data governance committee or forum in place, including remit and membership.
- Documented process for raising and resolving data quality or governance issues, and identified accountability at each step.
- Documented data domains subject to regulatory or compliance requirements and the individuals accountable for each.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Identify Data Domains and Ownership Responsibilities task are available.
- A stakeholder with responsibility for data governance is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Maturity and complexity of the customer's data governance framework
- Number of data domains subject to regulatory or compliance requirements
- Availability of existing governance documentation
- Number of stakeholders required for workshop
