# Software Engineer

The Software Engineer is a specialist role assigned to projects where the scope includes custom software development beyond the standard data platform work. This includes custom APIs, data ingestion connectors, embedded reporting applications, and customer-facing products built alongside the data solution. The Software Engineer works under the Data Lead alongside the Data Consultant and Data Engineer, engaging across all phases where custom development is in scope. The boundaries between the Software Engineer and Data Engineer will depend on the nature of the work and will be defined at the point of resourcing.

The Software Engineer role encompasses seniority levels from Software Engineer through to Senior Software Engineer. More senior engineers will typically take ownership of more complex development workloads and broader areas of the solution, while less experienced engineers may focus on specific components under the oversight of the Data Lead.

**Framework Involvement**

Design
- Contributes technical input across subjects where custom development is in scope, producing Design outputs for their area of work for review by the Data Lead.
- Works alongside the Data Consultant and Data Engineer to ensure custom development design decisions are consistent with the broader Design.

Platform
- Contributes to platform setup and configuration where custom development requirements affect the environment structure or deployment approach.

Build
- Leads the build of all custom software components confirmed in the agreed Design, including custom APIs, data ingestion connectors, embedded reporting applications, and customer-facing products where in scope.
- Conducts unit testing and peer review throughout, documenting outcomes and tracking any issues identified for resolution before the phase closes.
- Works alongside the Data Engineer where custom development and data engineering workloads intersect, with boundaries defined by the nature of the work.

Reporting
- Contributes to the Reporting phase where custom development is required to support report distribution, embedding, or application integration.

Quality Assurance
- Resolves defects identified in custom development components during testing, documenting outcomes and confirming resolution before the phase closes.

Release
- Validates that custom development outputs meet expectations in the production environment immediately following release and resolves any issues identified during the initial post-release period.
