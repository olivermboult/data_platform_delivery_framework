# Business User

The Business User is a frontline operational staff member who consumes reports and dashboards as part of their day to day work. They represent the end user perspective throughout the engagement, providing practical insight into how reporting is used, what works and what does not in the existing environment, and whether the solution meets their needs before it goes live. In some cases, the same individual may act as both a Business User and a Subject Matter Expert where they combine frontline reporting consumption with detailed knowledge of their area's business processes.

**Framework Involvement**

Pre-Discovery
- Contributes frontline context on reporting pain points, adoption challenges, and unmet needs during Reporting Initiation where their input is relevant to establishing the reporting scope.

Discovery
- Contributes frontline context on current report usage, adoption barriers, and self-service needs during Platform Discovery, helping the delivery team understand how reporting is consumed in practice across the business.
- Provides input on what questions reports must answer for their team and what information is most valuable to them day to day, informing storyboarding and wireframe design decisions during Business-Data Alignment.
- Contributes to any other end user facing Discovery sessions where frontline reporting consumption experience is relevant to the subject being explored.

Quality Assurance
- Participates in UAT as a nominated end user, validating that reports and dashboards meet their needs in practice across the agreed use cases.
- Confirms that reports within their area are fit for purpose and meet the success criteria agreed during Discovery.
- Provides structured feedback on usability, accuracy, and any issues identified during testing to support defect logging and resolution before the phase closes.

Release
- Receives release communication confirming the solution is live and accessible, including any guidance required to support initial adoption.
