# Data Owner

The Data Owner is a senior representative on the customer side, typically a department head or business unit leader, who holds ultimate accountability for a specific set of data within the organisation. They are responsible for the security, compliance, and appropriate use of data within their domain, and hold authority over who can access it and how it can be used. The Data Owner is not expected to be involved in the day to day detail of the project but must be available at key points to provide direction, confirm data governance decisions, and approve outputs that affect their domain.

**Framework Involvement**

Discovery
- Confirms the governance and access policies that apply to data within their domain, including any compliance or regulatory obligations that affect how the data can be used within the solution.
- Confirms whether any data sharing agreements or information governance approvals are required before data within their domain can be accessed or used by the project team.
- Provides direction on how data within their domain should be used, shared, and protected within the solution, including any sensitivity concerns that may affect architectural or provider decisions.
- Confirms data ownership responsibilities and accountability structures within their domain during Operating Model Discovery.
- Identifies any data access restrictions or security requirements that must be reflected in the solution design.
- Confirms the availability, accuracy, and suitability of data within their domain for the intended use cases during Source System Discovery and Business-Data Alignment sessions.

Design
- Reviews and approves data ownership and access control decisions within the operating model design, confirming that accountability structures and permission boundaries accurately reflect their domain's governance requirements.
- Confirms that data classification and protection decisions affecting their domain meet the organisation's compliance and regulatory obligations before build begins.
- Confirms who holds authority to approve access to data within their domain and ensures this is reflected in the roles and access design.

Build
- Remains available alongside the Data Steward during Build to confirm governance and compliance decisions where they arise during build.

Quality Assurance
- Confirms that data outputs within their domain meet the agreed governance and compliance requirements before the solution proceeds to release.

Live
- Participates in the post project review, providing input on whether data governance and ownership arrangements have operated effectively throughout the engagement.
