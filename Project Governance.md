# Project Governance

## Identify Project Stakeholders

**Activities**
- Identify all key stakeholders and document their roles, authority levels, and areas of responsibility.
- Identify the named Project Sponsor and document their authority to approve scope, budget, and escalations.
- Identify and document the primary point of contact on the customer side for day-to-day project communication.
- Identify any periods of stakeholder unavailability that may affect decision-making or delivery progress.
- Identify all third parties that will need to be engaged during or after the project, and document who on the customer side is responsible for managing each relationship.
- Capture any known differences in stakeholder priorities or expectations that may affect engagement or decision-making during the project.

**Outcomes**
- Documented list of key stakeholders, including roles, authority levels, and areas of responsibility.
- Identified named Project Sponsor and their authority to approve scope, budget, and escalations.
- Identified primary customer point of contact for day-to-day project communication.
- Documented periods of stakeholder unavailability that may affect decision-making or delivery progress.
- Documented third parties to be engaged during or after the project, including identified customer relationship owners.
- Documented known differences in stakeholder priorities or expectations.

**Pre-requisites**
- A stakeholder with organisational knowledge and authority to identify project roles is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of stakeholders involved in the project
- Number of third-party relationships to document
- Complexity of the stakeholder landscape

## Capture the Governance Structure

**Activities**
- Capture the governance framework the customer expects to follow, including how decisions will be made and how progress will be tracked.
- Document the expected escalation path for decisions that cannot be resolved within the delivery team, and identify who is responsible at each level.
- Document the authority levels required for different categories of decision, including changes to scope, budget, and timeline.
- Identify any governance requirements specific to the customer's organisation, such as steering committee reviews or board-level reporting.
- Identify any known constraints on decision-making, such as board approval cycles, procurement thresholds, or legal sign-off requirements.
- Identify any project management methodologies or internal standards the customer requires the project to follow.

**Outcomes**
- Documented governance framework the customer expects to follow, including decision-making and progress tracking processes.
- Documented escalation path for decisions outside the delivery team, including responsible parties at each level.
- Documented authority levels required for changes to scope, budget, and timeline.
- Documented governance requirements specific to the customer's organisation.
- Documented constraints on decision-making, such as board cycles or legal sign-off requirements.
- Documented project management methodologies or internal standards required for the project.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Identify Project Stakeholders task are available.
- Named stakeholders with governance responsibility are available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of the customer's governance structure
- Number of decision categories requiring defined authority levels
- Extent of organisation-specific governance requirements

## Capture Communication Requirements

**Activities**
- Capture the customer's preferred communication channels and document the individuals who should be included in project communications.
- Capture the expected meeting cadence, including the frequency and format of standups, progress reviews, and steering meetings.
- Capture the expected format and frequency of project status reporting, and identify the intended audience for each type of update.
- Identify any communication constraints, such as individuals or teams who should not receive certain categories of update.
- Capture the customer's requirements for how project documentation will be stored and shared, and identify who should have access.

**Outcomes**
- Documented preferred communication channels and identified individuals to be included in project communications.
- Documented meeting cadence, including frequency and format of standups, progress reviews, and steering meetings.
- Documented status reporting format and frequency, including the intended audience for each type of update.
- Documented communication constraints, including any restrictions on who receives certain categories of update.
- Documented requirements for project documentation storage and sharing, including access requirements.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Identify Project Stakeholders task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of distinct communication audiences
- Complexity of status reporting requirements
- Number of stakeholders with specific communication constraints

## Capture Risk and Change Management Requirements

**Activities**
- Identify whether the customer has an existing approach to risk and issue management, and capture any requirements for how risks and issues should be logged and owned during the project.
- Capture the customer's requirements and expectations around how changes to scope, timeline, or budget will be raised and approved, including the authority levels required for different categories of change.
- Identify whether the customer holds an existing risk register or issue log relevant to the project, and review any available documentation.
- Identify and document any known project risks or dependencies.

**Outcomes**
- Documented approach to risk and issue management, including requirements for logging and ownership.
- Documented change management requirements and approval authority levels for different categories of change.
- Documented existing risk register or issue log relevant to the project, where available.
- Documented known project risks and dependencies.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Capture the Governance Structure task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of the customer's existing risk and change management processes
- Number of known risks or dependencies to document
- Availability of existing risk registers or issue logs
