# Technical Stakeholder

The Technical Stakeholder is a senior technical representative on the customer side, typically a Head of IT, IT Manager, or Enterprise Architect. They are the primary source of technical information during Discovery and hold authority over technical sign-off at key points in the engagement. During technical sessions, the Technical Stakeholder interacts directly with the Data Engineer and I&S Engineer, with the Data Lead maintaining overall oversight of the relationship.

**Framework Involvement**

Pre-Discovery
- Acts as the primary source of information across infrastructure and security subjects during Platform, Data and AI Initiation, including existing platform configuration, software provider preferences, data protection requirements, and capacity management.
- Confirms the availability of technical documentation, system access, and personnel required to support Discovery.

Discovery
- Acts as the primary source of information across all infrastructure and security subjects during Platform Discovery, providing detail on the existing technical environment, security controls, compliance requirements, and operational practices.
- Contributes technical direction on platform, environment, and deployment decisions that will inform the Design.
- Confirms that technical information provided during Discovery sessions is accurate and complete before outputs are finalised.
- Consults on DevOps subjects within Operating Model Discovery, providing detail on existing deployment practices, source control, and change management processes.

Design
- Reviews and formally signs off all Design outputs covering platform architecture, environment strategy, security design, and infrastructure decisions before the Delivery stage begins.
- Remains available to the Data Engineer and I&S Engineer throughout Design to clarify technical requirements and validate design decisions.

Platform
- Reviews and formally confirms that the platform configuration meets the agreed Design before the platform readiness note is produced.

Quality Assurance
- Reviews and formally signs off the solution from a technical perspective, confirming that platform configuration, security controls, and infrastructure meet the agreed requirements before the solution proceeds to release.
