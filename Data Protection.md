# Data Protection

## Identify Personal Data and Classification Requirements

**Activities**
- Identify any personal data and special category data held within the source systems in scope, and document the data categories and any known volumes.
- Identify whether data masking or anonymisation will be required, and document the data domains or entities where this applies.
- Identify whether the customer has a data classification framework, and document whether it covers all data in scope.
- Identify whether data minimisation requirements apply, and document any constraints on what personal data can be collected or retained as part of the data flows in scope.

**Outcomes**
- Documented personal data and special category data held within the source systems in scope, including data categories and known volumes.
- Documented data masking or anonymisation requirements and the domains or entities where they apply.
- Documented data classification framework in place, including whether it covers all data in scope.
- Documented data minimisation requirements and any constraints on collection or retention of personal data within the data flows in scope.

**Pre-requisites**
- Named stakeholders with knowledge of the data held in source systems are available for a workshop.
- A Data Protection Officer or equivalent is available for a workshop where one is in place.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of source systems in scope containing personal or special category data
- Complexity of data classification requirements
- Extent of data masking or anonymisation requirements

## Identify Data Protection Regulatory Obligations

**Activities**
- Identify the data protection legislation that applies to the project, such as UK GDPR or the Data Protection Act 2018, and document the lawful basis for processing the personal data in scope.
- Identify any restrictions on where personal data can be stored or processed, including data residency and cross-border transfer requirements.
- Identify the roles of data controller and data processor for the data flows in scope, and document whether a data processing agreement is in place or will be required.
- Identify whether a Data Protection Impact Assessment will be required for any of the processing activities in scope, and document who is responsible for conducting it.
- Identify whether any data sharing agreements are in place that govern how source data can be used, stored, or processed, and document any relevant constraints.

**Outcomes**
- Documented data protection legislation applicable to the project and the lawful basis for processing each category of personal data in scope.
- Documented restrictions on where personal data can be stored or processed, including data residency and cross-border transfer requirements.
- Documented data controller and data processor roles for the data flows in scope, and whether a data processing agreement is in place or required.
- Identified whether a Data Protection Impact Assessment is required, and identified the responsible party.
- Documented data sharing agreements in place and any constraints they impose on how source data can be used, stored, or processed.

**Pre-requisites**
- A Data Protection Officer or named legal or compliance contact is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of data protection frameworks applicable to the project
- Number of jurisdictions in scope
- Complexity of data controller and processor arrangements
- Number of data sharing agreements to review

## Capture Retention and Data Subject Rights Requirements

**Activities**
- Capture the customer's current approach to data retention and deletion, including any obligations such as the right to erasure.
- Capture which data subject rights the solution will need to support, such as the right to access, rectification, and portability, and identify any processes already in place for handling these requests.

**Outcomes**
- Documented data retention and deletion approach, including any obligations under the right to erasure.
- Documented data subject rights the solution will need to support, and existing processes for handling subject access requests.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Identify Data Protection Regulatory Obligations task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of retention and deletion requirements
- Number of data subject rights the solution must support
- Extent of existing processes for handling data subject requests

---

## Identify Data Protection Governance

**Activities**
- Identify whether the customer has a Data Protection Officer or equivalent, and document whether their involvement will be required during the project.
- Identify whether data loss prevention policies are in place at the tenant level, and document any restrictions these impose on data movement or access.
- Identify whether the customer has a data breach or incident management process, and capture any requirements this places on the solution, such as audit logging, alerting, or notification workflows.

**Outcomes**
- Identified Data Protection Officer or equivalent, and documented whether their involvement will be required during the project.
- Documented data loss prevention policies in place and any restrictions they impose on data movement or access.
- Documented data breach and incident management process, and any requirements this places on the solution.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Identify Data Protection Regulatory Obligations task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity of data loss prevention policies in place
- Extent of requirements placed on the solution by breach or incident management processes
