# Strategic Objectives & Use Cases

## Capture Strategic Objectives and Programme Outcomes

**Activities**
- Identify and document the overarching business goals and strategic priorities the programme is intended to support.
- Capture any programme-level outcomes the customer expects to achieve, such as cost savings, efficiency improvements, revenue growth, or compliance requirements.
- Capture the timeframe and any fixed milestones associated with the stated objectives, and identify any deadlines that could affect scope or prioritisation.
- Review any existing strategy documents, business cases, or roadmaps the customer holds that define or inform the stated objectives.

**Outcomes**
- Documented overarching business goals and strategic priorities the programme is intended to support.
- Documented programme-level outcomes the customer expects to achieve.
- Documented timeframe, fixed milestones, and any deadlines that could affect scope or prioritisation.
- Documented findings from existing strategy documents, business cases, or roadmaps reviewed.

**Pre-requisites**
- A stakeholder with strategic authority and knowledge of programme objectives is available for a workshop.
- Relevant strategy documents, business cases, or roadmaps are accessible prior to the workshop where available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Complexity and breadth of the strategic objectives
- Availability of existing documentation to review
- Number of stakeholders required for workshop

## Identify Use Cases

**Activities**
- Identify the departments or areas of the business that are suitable sources of use cases.
- Identify who from each department will represent that area in discovery workshops, and document any gaps where representation cannot be confirmed.
- Capture a high-level overview of each potential use case, including the problem it addresses and the expected outcome.
- Review each identified use case against the customer's stated business objectives and document the degree of alignment, noting any use cases that appear misaligned or out of scope.

**Outcomes**
- Documented departments and business areas identified as suitable sources of use cases.
- Identified representatives for each department, and documented gaps where representation cannot be confirmed.
- Documented high-level overview of each potential use case, including the problem addressed and expected outcome.
- Documented alignment of each use case with the customer's strategic objectives, including any use cases identified as misaligned or out of scope.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Capture Strategic Objectives and Programme Outcomes task are available.
- Named representatives from relevant departments are available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of departments or business areas to engage
- Number of potential use cases identified
- Complexity of aligning use cases to strategic objectives

## Establish Use Case Detail

For each confirmed use case:

**Activities**
- Identify the key stakeholders, including the business owner, end users, and the individual with sign-off authority.
- Identify the pain points or operational challenges the use case is intended to resolve.
- Capture how the pain point or challenge is currently being handled, including any manual workarounds or interim processes in use.
- Capture the expected business value, such as time saved, cost reduced, risk mitigated, or improved decision-making.
- Capture the success criteria, covering both quantitative and qualitative outcomes.
- Review the captured success criteria with the relevant business owner, and record who will be responsible for validating them.
- Capture the customer's understanding of the scope boundary for the use case, including what is expected to be included and what is considered out of scope.
- Identify the data the customer believes is required for the use case and capture any known gaps or uncertainties around data availability.
- Identify any known technical constraints or system dependencies specific to the use case.

**Outcomes**
- Identified key stakeholders for each use case, including business owner, end users, and sign-off authority.
- Documented pain points and operational challenges each use case is intended to resolve.
- Documented how each challenge is currently handled, including any manual workarounds or interim processes.
- Documented expected business value for each use case.
- Documented success criteria for each use case, covering quantitative and qualitative outcomes, with identified validation responsibilities.
- Documented scope boundary for each use case, including what is in and out of scope.
- Documented data requirements for each use case and any known gaps or uncertainties around data availability.
- Documented technical constraints and system dependencies for each use case.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Identify Use Cases task are available.
- Business owners and end users for each confirmed use case are available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of confirmed use cases
- Complexity of each use case and its dependencies
- Number of stakeholders required per use case

## Review Previous Attempts and Existing Assets

**Activities**
- Document any use cases that have been attempted previously, including whether they succeeded or failed and the reasons for either outcome.
- Identify the pain points or operational challenges that drove any previous attempts, and document whether those challenges remain unresolved.
- Identify any existing data assets, reports, or tools developed during previous attempts that may be relevant to the current scope.

**Outcomes**
- Documented history of previous use case attempts, including outcomes and reasons for success or failure.
- Documented challenges that drove previous attempts and whether they remain unresolved.
- Documented existing data assets, reports, or tools from previous attempts that are relevant to the current scope.

**Pre-requisites**
- A stakeholder with knowledge of previous data or analytics initiatives is available for a workshop.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of previous use case attempts to review
- Availability of documentation from previous attempts
- Number of existing assets to identify and document

## Capture Use Case Prioritisation

**Activities**
- Identify any use cases driven by a regulatory, contractual, or time-bound obligation that would affect prioritisation.
- Identify any use cases that appear significantly more complex or resource-intensive than others and document any early indicators of scale.
- Identify any use cases that carry significant risk or uncertainty and document what is known about the nature of those risks.
- Identify any dependencies between use cases that could affect how they are sequenced or structured.
- Capture the customer's initial views on the relative priority of each use case, noting any that are considered urgent or strategically critical.
- Capture the customer's view of business readiness for each use case, including any known adoption challenges or change management concerns.
- Identify any risks that span multiple use cases or affect the programme as a whole, documenting these separately from individual use case risks.

**Outcomes**
- Documented use cases driven by regulatory, contractual, or time-bound obligations.
- Documented early indicators of scale or complexity for use cases identified as resource-intensive.
- Documented risks and uncertainties associated with individual use cases.
- Documented dependencies between use cases and any implied sequencing requirements.
- Documented customer's initial prioritisation of use cases, including any considered urgent or strategically critical.
- Documented business readiness and any known adoption challenges or change management concerns for each use case.
- Documented programme-level risks that span multiple use cases.

**Pre-requisites**
- Outputs from the Establish Use Case Detail task are available.

**Basis of Estimate**
- Number of use cases requiring prioritisation
- Complexity of dependencies between use cases
- Number of programme-level risks to document
